# Narrative Brief — amazon

## Confirmed answers

- **Narrative arc:** Opening (aerial establishing shot of the Amazon basin) → Act I: River & Jungle (the environment — water as spine, jungle as world) → Act II: Wildlife (the creatures that inhabit it — birds, mammals, reptiles, amphibians) → Act III: People & Community (the human life embedded in this ecosystem) → Closing frame (direct invitation to the viewer)
- **Key locations/subjects/moments:** Colombian Amazon; the river as primary transport artery and ecological spine; old-growth jungle canopy; local indigenous and riverside communities; diverse wildlife including pink river dolphin, howler monkey, squirrel monkey, sloth, hoatzin, Amazon kingfisher, red-and-black trogon, poison dart frog, yellow tree frog, giant spider; children and families living within the ecosystem
- **Audience:** Both personal followers and strangers — this is an invitation series addressed to anyone who has not yet visited the Colombian Amazon
- **Core takeaway:** The Colombian Amazon is a living world of extraordinary vibrance and diversity — a place of river, jungle, wildlife, and warm human community — worth visiting
- **Tone:** Warm lush editorial with documentary honesty. Rich color, immersive atmosphere, inviting rather than austere. The Amazon is not a threat to be endured — it is a world to be entered. Tone draws from the palette of the images themselves: deep greens, river silvers, golden dusk light, vivid animal color.
